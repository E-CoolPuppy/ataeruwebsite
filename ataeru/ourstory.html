our story.

what's it like to have allergies?

It’s honestly just annoying. Everywhere I go I have to call in advance or find the menu online and most times, restaurants will label things gluten free and then have cross contamination with crumbs all over their kitchen. I’m stuck in bed for a week because my fries were made in the same oil as chicken nuggets. I can’t eat real meals on planes when I travel and if I go on vacation I have to plan every meal in advance. Even after getting a reaction, my symptoms will gradually decrease over a couple of weeks and I won’t be able to have lactose for a month.

Grace is a 17-year-old girl whose had celiac disease for six months now, and clearly, it hasn't been easy. Celiac disease is an immune reaction to eating gluten, a protein found in things like bread, pasta, noodles, and much more. We fail to accommodate for the millions of people with food allergies in the world every day, forcing them to constantly be cautious for their own safety. Allergies were discovered over a hundred years ago, yet barely any progress has been made to find a cure.

something needed to change.

Together, the four of us – Grace, Alexis, Adam, and Jesse – banded together and founded ataeru, in order to allow people to eat freely and with confidence again. We hope that people like Grace will no longer have to worry about what’s in their food or feel painful symptoms for many weeks. They can sleep peacefully without nightmares of bread (yes, that actually happened to Grace) and explore foreign countries to their hearts’ content.

We’re determined to bring new solutions from the lab straight to your table, and it all starts here.
